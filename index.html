Why Another Flashcard App?

Hi, I’m Ilia — an indie developer passionate about language learning 👋
For the past four years, I’ve used Memrise to learn foreign languages like French 🇫🇷, German 🇩🇪, and Italian 🇮🇹 (my Memrise profile). However, Memrise announced that it will stop supporting user-created dictionaries in the near future. In response, I decided to create my own app called Flip, focused on learning - without the distractions of gamification or gimmicks.

Why Flip is Awesome?

Flip is built with the same core learning philosophy that made Memrise great, but structured in a way that keeps all your knowledge in one place rather than splitting it into multiple thematic sets.

🌍

30+ Languages Supported

Your language. Your choice. From Spanish and French to Japanese and Arabic, Flip supports over 30 languages to match your goals.

🌟

Spaced Repetition Algorithm

Flip uses a proven spaced repetition system to show you cards just before you’re about to forget them – maximizing retention while minimizing study time.

🧩

Different Learning Methods

Flip supports three effective learning methods to match your preferred style: choose the correct answer from six options, recall the translation from memory, or reverse the process by recalling the original word.

📚

Proofread Basic Set

Flip includes built-in basic dictionaries for popular languages, covering the most common words and phrases you need to get started. Perfect for beginners or those building a solid foundation before diving deeper.

Pay Only For Result

Flip is a results-based paid app. You’re not paying for your time or the app itself – only for the cards you master. If Flip doesn’t help you memorize a card, you don’t pay.

How does it works?

At the start, Flip gives you 100 Premium cards for FREE. When a card reaches Level 5 (after a 12-day successful repetition period), it begins to count toward your Premium balance. Once your balance runs out, you’ll need to top up to keep learning.

💎 100 Premium cards – $1.99
💎 1000 Premium cards – $9.99

Once purchased, Premium cards never expire – your total balance only grows.

How many words do I need for daily conversations?

In general, knowledge of the most frequent 3,000 words in a language is sufficient to understand 85–90% of daily conversations, media, and written texts.
