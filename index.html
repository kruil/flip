How Spaced Repetition Works

Memorila - Flashcards Reinvented

Download on the App Store

Review each card just before you forget it

In the 1880s, German psychologist Hermann Ebbinghaus showed that memories fade quickly without reinforcement. Spaced repetition turns this forgetting curve into a learning schedule – new words reappear soon, then at gradually longer intervals.

If you remember a card, it appears less often. If you forget it, it returns sooner. This adapts to your memory in real time, saving time and helping vocabulary move into long-term memory.

Different Learning Methods

Memorila supports multiple learning methods that adapt to your progress. Each card has its own learning level, which you can adjust at any time manually or automatically.

Choose the answer

Start here when learning new words. Best for unfamiliar vocabulary and rare expressions.

Recall translation

Upgrade when you can reliably recognize the meaning and choose the correct translation without hesitation.

Recall Original Word

Use this level when you want active recall for real communication. At this stage, you should be able to produce the word yourself.

Word Explanation 💫

Understand nuances, compare similar words!

Not sure which word fits? Explanation compares similar words, explains the difference between answers with the same translation, and shows examples and usage notes.

Walking Mode🏃‍♂️‍➡️

Turn your daily walk into a lesson!

Walking Mode lets you repeat cards while you’re on the move – no need to stop or even look at the screen! Memorila reads the question aloud. Tap once to reveal and hear the answer. Tap again to mark the card as remembered, or double tap if you forgot it.
